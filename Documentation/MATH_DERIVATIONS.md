# WYVERN-E 4.0 — Ground-Test Rig Math Derivations

This document derives the math the two ground-test-stand sketches
(`wyvern4_gse_servo_rig/` and `wyvern4_gse_solenoid_rig/`) depend on: the **3-axis load-cell
calibration matrix** (both rigs), which resolves the full thrust vector and hence the gimbal
deflection directly from the balance. (The earlier ToF-ring plane-fit + Kalman-fusion approach has
been removed from the design — the ToF sensors were dropped; gimbal deflection now comes from the
load balance plus the gimbal BNO085.) All numeric values below are reproduced exactly by
`derive_math.py` in this folder — re-run it if any of the physical constants (load-cell full-scale,
lever arms, sensor noise) change on the bench.

Companion reading: `../../Documentation/WYVERN_E4_GSE_TestStands.md` (rig mechanical description),
`../../Documentation/CONFLICTS.md` (frozen flight parameters this project also carries), and
`../../Documentation/PID_TUNING_REPORT.md` (the flight-control-loop derivation this rig's data
ultimately feeds into, since one of its jobs is validating actuator bandwidth/slew assumptions used
in that tuning).

## 1. Load-cell calibration matrix (both TVC-balance rigs)

### 1.1 Why a full 3×3 matrix, not three independent single-axis gains

Per `GSE_TestStands.md` §1, the actuator under test bolts to a thrust block restrained by three
strain-gauge load cells through flexures: one axial (Z, 5 kg) and two lateral (X, Y, 1 kg each),
each read through its own HX711 24-bit ADC. The naive model treats each cell as reporting only the
force along its own axis, i.e. a diagonal calibration ("X counts → Fx only"). Real flexure-mounted
stands don't behave that way: no physical flexure is a perfect single-degree-of-freedom joint, so an
axial load also deflects the lateral flexures slightly (and a lateral load couples a little into the
axial cell). Fitting a **dense 3×3 sensitivity matrix** captures this cross-axis coupling instead of
silently absorbing it as calibration error.

### 1.2 Measurement model

$$c_i = c_{0,i} + \sum_{j\in\{x,y,z\}} S_{ij} F_j, \qquad i \in \{x,y,z\}\text{-cell}$$

or in matrix form, $\mathbf{c} = \mathbf{c}_0 + S\mathbf{F}$, where $S$ is 3×3 (counts/N) and
$\mathbf{c}_0$ is the 24-bit HX711 tare/zero-load offset (one per channel).

### 1.3 Calibration procedure and least-squares fit

Apply $N \ge 4$ **known** force vectors $\mathbf{F}^{(k)}$ — single-axis hanging masses along each
of X, Y, Z plus a few off-axis/combined-load points for redundancy — and record the resulting raw
counts $\mathbf{c}^{(k)}$. Stack into the augmented design matrix

$$A = \begin{bmatrix}F_x^{(1)} & F_y^{(1)} & F_z^{(1)} & 1\\ \vdots & & & \vdots\\ F_x^{(N)} & F_y^{(N)} & F_z^{(N)} & 1\end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{N\times 4}$$

and solve for each channel's row of $[S_{i,:}\ \ c_{0,i}]$ via the Moore–Penrose pseudo-inverse:

$$\theta_i = A^{+}\mathbf{c}_i, \qquad A^{+} = (A^\top A)^{-1}A^\top \text{ (or `np.linalg.pinv`)}$$

This pseudo-inverse least-squares solve is a small fixed-size `pinv3()`/`lstsq` helper shared by
both DAQ sketches (servo-rig and solenoid-rig balances use the identical calibration primitive).

**Validation (`derive_math.py`).** A synthetic ground-truth $S_\text{true}$ with realistic
cross-axis terms (~1% of the diagonal) was used to generate 9 calibration points (1 tare + 4
single-axis + 2 off-axis) at 15-count (1σ) HX711 noise. The pseudo-inverse fit recovered $S$ to
within **0.6 counts/N** of the true matrix (diagonal terms ~8500–42000 counts/N) — negligible next
to the ~1% assumed cross-coupling itself. The fitted $S$:

```
S_fit (counts/N) =
[[ 8499.7   120.1   -80.3]
 [  100.4  8499.7    59.5]
 [  -50.0    40.3 41999.4]]
cond(S_fit) = 5.01        (well-conditioned — cross-coupling terms are small next to the diagonal)
eig(S_fit)  = [8389.7, 8609.5, 41999.6]   (all real, all positive → S is invertible and physical)
```

### 1.4 Runtime force reconstruction and thrust-vector angles

At runtime, invert once (`S_inv = np.linalg.inv(S_fit)`, or the equivalent fixed 3×3 closed-form
inverse baked into firmware) and recover force per sample:

$$\hat{\mathbf{F}} = S^{-1}(\mathbf{c} - \mathbf{c}_0)$$

then the thrust vector magnitude/direction from `GSE_TestStands.md` §1:

$$T=\sqrt{F_x^2+F_y^2+F_z^2},\quad \theta=\operatorname{atan2}\!\left(\sqrt{F_x^2+F_y^2},\,F_z\right),\quad \phi=\operatorname{atan2}(F_y,F_x)$$

(`atan2` form used for $\theta$ rather than a bare `atan` so it stays well-defined as $F_z\to 0$.)
A synthetic test point ($F=(7.85,\,-5.88,\,20.0)$ N through the noisy fit) reconstructed to within
**0.5 mN** on each axis and **0.001°** on $\theta,\phi$ — the fit is not the dominant error source;
raw per-sample HX711 noise (~15 counts ≈ 1.5–2 mN on the 1 kg cells) is.

## 2. Reproducibility

All numbers in this document are generated by `derive_math.py` in this folder and cross-checked in
`phase0_math_constants.json` (machine-readable form of every matrix/scalar above, consumed by the
DAQ firmware's calibration-constant header comments and by any later re-derivation). Re-run the
script after changing: load-cell full-scale, lever arms/flexure geometry, assumed HX711/BNO085 noise
figures, or the loop rate.
